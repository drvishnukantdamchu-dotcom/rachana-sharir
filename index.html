About the Department

Bridging Classical Ayurvedic Anatomy with Modern Anatomical Sciences

The Department of Rachana Sharir (Human Anatomy) at BBSPM's Dhanwantari Ayurved Medical College & Hospital, Udgir, is one of the fundamental pre-clinical departments established since the inception of the college in the year 2000.

Our department follows the NCISM 2021 syllabus pattern (Subject Code: AyUG-RS) with a total of 500 teaching hours comprising 180 Lecture Hours and 320 Non-Lecture Hours. The college is affiliated to MUHS, Nashik and approved by NCISM, New Delhi.

From the academic year 2025-26, the intake capacity has been increased from 60 to 100 students. The department is equipped with a modern Dissection Hall, a rich Museum, digital learning tools, and the premium Complete Anatomy (Elsevier) software worth ₹4,00,000 annual subscription.

The college campus sprawls over 7 acres with a digital library of 10,000+ books and a verdant herbal garden with 260+ species and 850+ plants.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To establish the department as a Centre of Excellence in Ayurvedic anatomical education, research, and training. To provide health service and training in the field of Ashtang Ayurveda, keeping pace with modern medicine and techniques. "EFFORT IS GOD!!!"

Our Mission

  • To impart practical and clinical-based knowledge of Rachana Sharir and Anatomy.
  • To promote innovation in teaching through PBL, CBL, ECE, Virtual Dissection, and cadaveric dissection.
  • To promote organ donation awareness and community health education.
  • To promote research activities and validate Ayurveda modalities.
  • To empower learners with best medical education coupled with professional skills.
