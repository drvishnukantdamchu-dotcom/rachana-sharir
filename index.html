About the Department

Shaping the future of Ayurvedic anatomical education with modern tools and traditional wisdom

The Department of Rachana Sharir (Human Anatomy) at BBSPM's Dhanwantari Ayurved Medical College, Udgir, is one of the fundamental pre-clinical departments dedicated to imparting comprehensive knowledge of human anatomy integrated with Ayurvedic principles as described in classical texts.

Our department follows the NCISM 2021 syllabus pattern and is committed to delivering quality education through a blend of traditional Ayurvedic anatomical concepts (Shareera Rachana) and modern anatomical sciences. From the academic year 2025-26, the intake capacity has been increased from 60 to 100 students, reflecting the growing trust and demand for our educational excellence.

The department is well-equipped with a modern Dissection Hall featuring 1 Demo Station and 4 Practice Stations, a rich Museum housing specimens, models, and charts, along with state-of-the-art digital learning tools including the premium Complete Anatomy (Elsevier) software subscription worth ₹4,00,000 annually.

We actively engage in community outreach through Organ Donation Awareness programs, health awareness campaigns, school visits, exhibitions, rallies, and street plays (Pathnatya), contributing to societal well-being beyond the classroom.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To be a centre of excellence in Ayurvedic anatomical education and research, bridging the classical Shareera Rachana knowledge with modern anatomical sciences, and producing competent Ayurvedic physicians who understand the structural foundation of the human body in both traditional and contemporary perspectives.

Our Mission

To provide high-quality anatomical education through innovative teaching methodologies, hands-on dissection training, digital learning tools, and integration of Ayurvedic and modern anatomical concepts. To promote organ donation awareness, community health education, and research in the field of Rachana Sharir. To nurture students who are skilled in understanding human structural organization for better clinical practice.

Department Facilities & Features

State-of-the-art infrastructure for comprehensive anatomical education

Dissection Hall

Well-equipped hall with 1 Demo Station and 4 Practice Stations for hands-on cadaveric dissection training as per NCISM guidelines.

Anatomy Museum

A comprehensive museum housing 204 specimens, 237 models, and 151 charts covering all systems of the human body.

Complete Anatomy (Elsevier)

Premium 3D anatomy software with annual subscription worth ₹4,00,000 — providing students with interactive digital dissection experience.

Projector & AV Room

Dedicated room with projector facility for PowerPoint presentations, video lectures, and multimedia-based teaching.

Department Library

Collection of 81 reference books including classical Ayurvedic texts and modern anatomy textbooks for student reference.

Digital Learning Tools

Access to Visible Body Atlas, 3D4Medical Anatomy, Ayurvedic Rachana Sharir App, and BioDigital Human software for enhanced learning.
