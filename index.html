About the Department

Bridging Classical Ayurvedic Anatomy with Modern Anatomical Sciences

The Department of Rachana Sharir (Human Anatomy) at BBSPM's Dhanwantari Ayurved Medical College & Hospital, Udgir, is one of the fundamental pre-clinical departments established since the inception of the college in the year 2000. The department is dedicated to imparting comprehensive knowledge of human anatomy integrated with Ayurvedic principles as described in classical texts of Sushruta, Charaka, and Vagbhata.

Our department follows the NCISM 2021 syllabus pattern (Subject Code: AyUG-RS) with a total of 500 teaching hours comprising 180 Lecture Hours (LH) and 320 Non-Lecture Hours (NLH) including practicals and dissection. The college is affiliated to Maharashtra University of Health Sciences (MUHS), Nashik and approved by NCISM, New Delhi.

From the academic year 2025-26, the intake capacity has been increased from 60 to 100 students, reflecting the growing trust and demand for our educational excellence. The department is equipped with a modern Dissection Hall, a rich Museum, digital learning tools, and the premium Complete Anatomy (Elsevier) software.

The college campus sprawls over 7 acres and includes classrooms equipped with audio-visual tools, a digital library with more than 10,000 books, various laboratories, and a verdant herbal garden with over 260 species and about 850 plants.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To be a centre of excellence in Ayurvedic anatomical education and research, establishing structural parameters of the human body as per both Ayurvedic and modern aspects. To produce competent Ayurvedic physicians who understand Rachana Sharir as the foundation for clinical practice, diagnosis, and management of diseases.

Our Mission

  • To impart practical and clinical-based knowledge of Rachana Sharir and Anatomy strengthening students' foundation of basic Ayurvedic principles.
  • To provide thorough understanding of gross and microscopic structure and development of human body in perspective of ancient and modern sciences.
  • To promote innovation in teaching through didactic, demonstration, PBL, CBL, ECE, Virtual Dissection, and cadaveric dissection methods.
  • To enhance practical applicability of Rachana Sharir including Marma Sharir and Srotas concepts.
  • To promote organ donation awareness and community health education.

Department Facilities & Infrastructure

State-of-the-art infrastructure for comprehensive anatomical education

Dissection Hall

Well-equipped hall with 1 Demo Station and 4 Practice Stations for hands-on cadaveric dissection training as per NCISM guidelines. Students learn Mruta Samshodhana as described by Sushruta.

Anatomy Museum

A comprehensive museum housing 204 Specimens, 237 Models, and 151 Charts covering all systems — Osteology, Arthrology, Myology, Nervous System, Viscera, and more.

Complete Anatomy (Elsevier)

Premium 3D anatomy software with annual subscription worth ₹4,00,000 providing students with interactive digital dissection, clinical correlations, and 3D animated anatomy.

Projector & AV Room

Dedicated room with projector facility for PowerPoint presentations, 3D animated videos, educational films, and multimedia-based teaching as per NCISM teaching methods.

Department Library

Collection of 81 reference books including classical Ayurvedic texts (Sushruta Samhita, Charaka Samhita, Ashtanga Hridaya) and modern anatomy textbooks.

Digital Learning Tools

Access to Visible Body Atlas, 3D4Medical Anatomy, Ayurvedic Rachana Sharir App, and BioDigital Human software for enhanced digital learning experience.
